Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the budget simulator? +
Our budget simulator uses your exact inputs to calculate your monthly balance, savings rate, and spending breakdown in real time. It's designed as an educational tool to help you visualize your finances — for official financial planning, always consult a certified advisor.
Is my financial data saved? +
No. All calculations happen directly in your browser. We never store, collect or transmit your financial data. You can use our tools with complete peace of mind.
What is a good savings rate? +
Financial experts generally recommend saving at least 10-20% of your net income. The popular 50/30/20 rule suggests 50% for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings and debt repayment. Even starting with 5% and increasing gradually makes a big difference over time.
How do I improve my Financial IQ score? +
Read the explanations after each quiz question — they're designed to teach, not just test. Focus on understanding compound interest, emergency funds, investment basics, and debt management. Retake the quiz after a week to track your improvement.
What's the difference between gross and net income? +
Gross income is your total earnings before taxes and deductions. Net income (take-home pay) is what you actually receive after all deductions. Always use your net income when budgeting — it's your real spending power.
